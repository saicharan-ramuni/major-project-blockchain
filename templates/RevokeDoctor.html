Manage Doctor Access

Control which doctors can decrypt and view your health reports. This uses the KAC-UR key revocation scheme.

How KAC-UR Revocation Works

When you revoke a doctor's access, the system uses the Key-Aggregate Cryptosystem with User Revocation (Liu et al., IEEE TKDE 2024) to advance to a new time period. The doctor's existing Update Keys become cryptographically invalid — even if they have old keys cached, they cannot generate new valid keys and therefore cannot decrypt your reports.

Revocation is Permanent

Once you revoke a hospital's access, they will not be able to download or decrypt any of your reports. To restore access, you would need to re-share your reports with them. This action is recorded immutably on the blockchain.
